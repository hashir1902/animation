What is Animation?

Animation is the art of bringing images to life. In 2D animation, flat characters and environments are manipulated frame by frame to simulate movement and emotion.

Whether you're drawing on paper, using software like Stick Nodes or Adobe Animate, the core concept remains the same: motion through sequence.

2D animation is used in cartoons, explainer videos, games, and much more. It's a blend of creativity, storytelling, and technical skill.

Complete Guide to 2D Animation

  • 1. Understanding Timing & Spacing: These are the building blocks of realistic motion.
  • 2. Tools of the Trade: Use software like Stick Nodes, Adobe Animate, Toon Boom Harmony, or free tools like Pencil2D.
  • 3. Keyframes and Inbetweens: Learn how to structure animations using main poses and transition frames.
  • 4. Storyboarding: Plan your scenes visually before animating.
  • 5. Animation Principles: Master squash & stretch, anticipation, follow-through, and more from the 12 principles of animation.
  • 6. Audio Syncing: Learn how to lip-sync dialogue and match sound effects.
  • 7. Exporting and Sharing: How to render, compress, and share your work on social media or YouTube.
  • 8. Practice: Consistent daily practice helps you improve faster than any tool or course.
